Comparative research protocol

1. Identify the substrate

Locate the physical, historical or institutional boundary that may have structured later development.

2. Reconstruct the mechanism

Trace the intermediate economic, demographic and institutional processes rather than jumping from an old map to a modern outcome.

3. Test alternatives

Compare plausible controls, boundary zones and periods of disruption. State uncertainty and evidence strength.

4. Translate carefully

Use the result for territorial intelligence, institutional analysis and field research—not for essentialising populations.
